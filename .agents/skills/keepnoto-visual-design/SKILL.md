# Keepnoto Visual Design

Use this skill as Keepnoto's product-specific source of truth. Let general design skills raise craft quality, but let this skill decide whether the result feels like Keepnoto.

## Product Feel

Keepnoto should feel like a personal knowledge vault: calm, warm, intentional, and quietly premium. Saved links should feel like meaningful artifacts the user chose to keep, not rows in a bookmark manager.

Design toward:

- Modern, warm, calm, premium but simple.
- Soft gradients that add atmosphere without taking over the interface.
- Bento cards for saved links, collections, summaries, notes, and high-value groupings.
- Strong typography hierarchy with confident titles, readable metadata, and restrained supporting text.
- Clean spacing with enough room for scanning, comparing, and returning to saved material.
- A sense of curation, memory, and personal value.

## Visual Direction

Prefer warm neutrals, ink-like text, muted accent colors, and soft depth. Use gradients as gentle environmental tone: sunrise warmth, paper-like light, subtle dusk, or quiet library energy.

Use bento layouts when they help links feel curated:

- Feature important saves with richer cards.
- Let collections feel like shelves, stacks, or personal vault sections.
- Use metadata, tags, source, date, excerpt, and notes as supporting hierarchy rather than clutter.
- Give saved links enough visual presence to feel worth revisiting.

Keep the interface simple:

- Prioritize one strong focal point per view.
- Use spacing, scale, and type before borders.
- Prefer soft surfaces, tonal separation, and shadow only when it clarifies structure.
- Keep controls obvious but visually quiet.

## Typography

Use typography to make the product feel premium and trustworthy.

- Make primary headings decisive and noticeably larger than body text.
- Make saved-link titles strong, readable, and valuable-feeling.
- Keep metadata and helper text legible; never let secondary text become low contrast.
- Avoid weak, same-size type stacks where everything competes.
- Use line length and spacing to make dense saved content comfortable to scan.

## Cards And Saved Links

Saved links are the core emotional surface of Keepnoto. Design them as saved knowledge objects.

Good saved-link cards:

- Have clear title hierarchy and useful preview content.
- Surface source, domain, tags, collection, note, or summary with restraint.
- Use image thumbnails, favicons, color marks, or soft accents when they add recognition.
- Feel calm and collectible, not noisy.
- Make actions available without turning every card into a control panel.

Avoid turning saved links into a plain table, generic feed, or cramped bookmark list unless the user explicitly needs a dense management mode.

## Avoid

- Generic blue SaaS visuals.
- Childish UI, toy-like colors, or novelty styling.
- Boring bookmark-manager layouts.
- Random glassmorphism with no product reason.
- Overcomplicated dashboards.
- Weak typography hierarchy.
- Low contrast text.
- Too many borders.
- Noisy cards, excessive badges, or decorative clutter.

## Review Checklist

Before finishing Keepnoto UI work, verify:

- The screen feels warm, calm, modern, and premium but simple.
- The design reads as a personal knowledge vault.
- Saved links feel valuable and revisitable.
- Typography hierarchy is strong and readable.
- Spacing is clean and intentional.
- Gradients, bento cards, shadows, borders, and accents serve the content.
- The result avoids generic blue SaaS and boring bookmark-manager patterns.
